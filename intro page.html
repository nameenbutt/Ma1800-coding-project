Faith & Fire – Introduction

Notebook Entries

The notebook began long before the war. Before the dust. Before the country split itself into shadow and prayer.

“I write so nothing disappears,” she wrote.

On the first faded page, she left no dates. Only a sentence:

“If you found this, the story is already yours.”

A corner of the page folds toward the first entry.

Another crease leads toward the years she feared.

A faint margin note points to the echoes she saved.

“What she remembered most were the colours.”

She never meant this to be read in order. Every page spills into another.

If you prefer faith over memory,
follow her to the page she guarded.

If you seek your own meaning,
leave a trace in the blank space she left for you.
